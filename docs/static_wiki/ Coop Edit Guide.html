Unique to the Cube engines , Coop Edit lets you build and modify maps with others seamlessly.
This "game mode" just allows you to switch to the edit mode online , at the cost of some missing functions.
For instance , you can't undo any change. It would not make sense when several people modify the same cubes.
Map changes are visible to all connected players , but joining in the middle of a coopedit section may be confusing , you could find yourself playing on the previous version of a map. To save your progress , type /sendmap to upload the current map to the server and others can use /getmap to edit and play on the same version. You can't undo changes , but you can revert the map back to its previous state on your site by downloading it from the server again.

Lighting is disabled by default on coopedit (fullbright on) so that you are not left in the dark when starting a new map. It is possible to enable it again and recalculate lighting , but this is a client-side calculation , only you will see the map lit properly. Still , lights and other entities are saved with the map when you upload it.

Remember that you and other players have absolute control over the map , this is cooperative editing - you can add to other's work or remove it. It is poor form to delete or "overwrite" other's work without their consent , just like it wasn't nice to destroy the other kid's castle in the sandbox. Coop editors try to not work next to each other to avoid interference.

Coopediting can speed up repetitive or boring processes especially for large and complicated maps , like smoothing lots of curves or building long stairways. Other editors can fix errors that you didn't notice with a method you didn't know about. It is important in coopedit to share editing techniques , so that even newbies can help you out. Think of editing Cube maps as writing wiki articles : anyone can contribute , and even if suffering some damage (inflicted deliberately or not) it can be easily reverted back to a normal state.
