Multiplayer Introduction

Before you start to play on the interwebs you should have taken some time to setup your game.
Apart from having an appropriate graphics setup for maximum fps to keep the game fluid while online (see Performace Guide) you should have changed your name and team in your autoexec.cfg.
When running the game you can go to the multiplayer menu, get an update from the master server and will then see the currently running games available to join to.
The server browser displays enough basic information about a respective game to help you choose the right server for yourself.
Take note of indicators for private and locked mastermodes ("P" or "L" next to number of players).

The game runs in a range of different modes.
Internally the are mapped to the indexes [-2 .. 0 .. 12], with the <0 modes being the SP modes.
The current modes are :
  • 0 : ffa/default
  • 1 : coopedit
  • 2 : ffa/duel
  • 3 : teamplay
  • 4 : instagib
  • 5 : instagib team
  • 6 : efficiency
  • 7 : efficiency team
  • 8 : insta arena
  • 9 : insta clan arena
  • 10 : tactics arena
  • 11 : tactics clan arena
  • 12 : capture

Do not worry, there a some nice aliases defined to keep you from remembering these numbers off by heart.
There are far more modes available than in most FPS games, so there should be a type of game for any player preference.

When playing online you can change the current mode and current map being played by voting for a new game.
This is done either via the menu or by simply typing the command to the console (which is easier to show here).
/insta curvedm
or in full :
/mode 4; map curvedm
will vote for an instagib match on the map "Curves That Kill".
Votes are passed either by >50% agreement from all players or by the master forcing the vote (see mastermodes [TODO:link]

If you are voting for a team mode it is strongly suggested to take care of an appropriate team setup (balanced?) before passing the vote!
In team mode your mates are teinted green - so only shoot the Ogro's that look the same as ever!

The modes all have their own little special flavour so you have, in theory, 15 different games instead of just one ;)
With exception of the Capture mode you can play any mode on any of the maps available, although some may be better suited to one mode more than another.
In the FFA modes everything is "as usual" with items spawning and respawning and players just fragging away. Actually ffa/default and ffa/duel aren't really any different modes.
Instagib is your classic 1-shot-1-kill type of game, no items spawn in this mode - it's just movement and aiming that can keep you alive longer than your enemy. Players respawn when being killed. Your only weapons are a rifle with 100 shots, and your fist - which does for quick frags when up close to your enemy, since it's "rate of fire" is more rapid.
In the efficiency modes you spawn with a random subset of ammunition, thus giving you a temporary assignment (especially in team mode) or simply forcing a certain tactical approach on you. Players respawn when being killed.
The arena modes differ from their respective basic modes (tactics is based on efficiency) by players not respawning directly after being fragged.
Rather each round will only end when a last-man-standing has beaten all other players, then a new round starts.
Currently you are completely motionless when fragged and waiting for a new round to start.
Finally the capture mode has a completely different approach: for starters, frags are not counted at all.
The goal is to keep certain bases under your control for as long as possible, this grants points per team.
When you die you will stay in limbo for 10 seconds before attempting to spawn next to one of your teams bases.
You have lost if you have no bases captured at this point or when your opposing team manages to hold all bases at any one moment in time.


Hosting a Server


Setup

If you want to host your own server you should be sure to have setup your network accordingly.
To enable access from a WAN (read internet) your routing needs to forward the games ports (see below);
for LAN gaming see the following paragraph.

Player VS. Speed

A home DSL connection upstream is rumoured to be sufficient to host a 4-player-server, this is currently standard for a server without stating another number in the passed parameters.


Configuration

For a complete list of parameters always check the documentation (doc/config.html Section:"command line options").
If you are unsure as how to setup your network & firewall correctly here are some useful hints.
We can't explain every little detail about this complex subject and encourage you to use the rest of the interwebs for additional help!
Take note of possibility to set a master-password,
which will give you master even though somebody else already is master. Because you know the servers password. Use comand line param -p$$$ for this - with $$$ your password - this is never sent to other clients.


PUBLIC or PRIVATE

If you do not want your server to be listed on the masterserver's list - which everybody gets when updating their list -
then simply point it to a non-working masterserver, most common would be -mlocalhost.


Router & Ping Responce

On the forum a lot of router issues were resolved by pointing to portforward.com,
sadly a number of ISPs seem to hand out routers that are incapable of forwarding ports - if you have been given such a device you will, sadly, not be able to host a server.
The ports you need to forward (or DNAT) to your local box (running the server) are
28785 and 28786. Only UDP is sufficient.
TCP is only used for outgoing connections via port 80 for the masterserver update of the client.
If you get a message like "server did not respond to ping" your ports aren't being forwarded correctly yet.


Playing over LAN

On your LAN the limit of players will probably depend on processing power of the hosting server.
The clients can use
/lanconnect
to connect to this server easily; to easily manage this with large number of players you might take some time to keybind and/or integrate this into the menu for all players.
Look at scripting/menu modification documentation (as soon as it's written) for help on this.
